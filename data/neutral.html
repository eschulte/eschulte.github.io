neutrality and selection

Variables

N
population size
Ne
effective population size
S
selection coefficient

Statements

  • when NeS << 1 the fate of mutations is determined by drift, so in small populations the stochastic effects of drift overcome the effects of selection

Examples

  • Biological (Eyre-Walker & Keightley, 2007)
    • drosophila
      • Ne ≅ 106
      • ~50% of the fixed non-synonymous substitutions are selective
      • ~16% of non-synonymous mutations are effectively neutral
    • hominids
      • Ne ≅ 104
      • ~0% of the fixed non-synonymous substitutions are selective
      • ~30% of non-synonymous mutations are effectively neutral
  • Computational (Schulte)
    • Siemens (very well tested programs)
      • ~20% of mutations to tested portions of the source code are neutral
    • Programs in general (less thoroughly tested)
      • ~35% of mutations to tested portions of the source code are neutral
